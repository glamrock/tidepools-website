Collaborative Time-Based Community-Centric Social Mapping Platform

Multi-platform, Re-Skinnable, Customizable, Mesh Friendly

+
Community Design Process

Great tools are driven by community needs. Tidepools emerged from many brainstorming sessions with community members in Red Hook, Brooklyn and has continued to evolve as it has been re-skinned and modified by other communities to suit their needs.

+
In-Depth Customization

Tidepools is designed to be modular so that it is very easy to customize and re-configure to meet the needs of the community who wants to use it. Check out the variations of Tidepools - "In The Wild" above to see how Tidepools is used in Red Hook, Brooklyn, or for Gezi Square in Turkey or for the AMC Conference in Detroit.

+
Rich Interaction

Unlike many mapping tools, which require users to fill out a form, or have location services turned on, Tidepools allows users to interact via drag and drop. Users can engage in threaded conversations about multiple types of content and add to the map via SMS text. All content is stored not just by location but also time, allowing landmarks to grow and change with fluctuating activity.

Overview

Tidepools is a re-skinnable collaborative mobile mapping platform for gathering and sharing hyperlocal information and culture through expressive, community maps and data feeds, enabling greater connectivity and communication among neighbors and local organizations.

Tidepools bridges the digital and physical space of a neighborhood, storing its data on local servers and broadcasted over WiFi so it can run even without reliance on an Internet connection. It integrates location-specific civic data in situ, including real time transit notifications and community safety issues. Community members can share events, user-created map layers and landmarks, and other local temporal information, creating a historical geospatial community database.


Current Apps

+
Status / SMS

In support of relief efforts and needs during Hurricane Sandy, the team added the ability to SMS text to the map, so that an internet connection nor a smart phone were needed to allow residents to send needs, issues information to the volunteer team. The residents phone numbers are protected using encyption, texts are geocoded using natural language locations, and conversations are threaded related to allow users monitoring the map to maintain contact with an SMS contributor.

+
Where's the B61 Bus?

The only transit available within Red Hook are two bus lines, and as is the case with many of the busses in NYC, the busses are frequently late or off their schedule. One of the busses, the B61, now has real time information and tracking as part of the NYC MTA's Bustime project. "Where's the B61?" is a localized app that provides information just about the B61 for residents. The App is available over the wifi, so that residents only need a wifi-enabled device to access the information.

+
Stop & Frisk

The NYPD Stop, Question, and Frisk program has been the subject of controversy around the city and is currently the subject of active litigation. Residents in Red Hook, Brooklyn were concerned about activity in the neighborhood and wanted a way to collect their own data to compare with the NYPD reports. This app allows the residents to report if incidents happen to them or if they see incidents occur. All of the data is stored locally and owned by the community.


Coming Soon

+
APIs, APIs, APIs

We're making it easier to pull in your favorite web and social media content. Although Tidepools is designed to be a local community tool, we want to be compatible with existing tools that various communities use, such as Twitter, Instagram, Tumblr, Flickr, Vine, etc etc. We are working on an easy plug & play interface to add the external systems you want to your local data ecosystem.

+
Multi-platform User Interface: Mobile, Table, & Desktop

The customizable Tidepools interface will have a mobile, tablet and desktop interfaces, allowing users to interact with the tool from a variety of devices.

+
Integrates with local weather, timezone day & nights, seasons

The Tidepools environment is response to the weather, time and seasons, allowing community members to experience information not just in locational context but also in a temporal context. Tidepools will also be able to collect local environmental data via sensors.
